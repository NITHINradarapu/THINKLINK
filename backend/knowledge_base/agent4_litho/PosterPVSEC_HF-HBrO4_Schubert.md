# **Motivation** 

**Generated Surface Morphologies** 

- Aqueous solutions of bromine (Br2), hydrofluoric acid (HF) & 





   - hydrobromic acid (HBr) are suitable for (an)isotropic etching<sup>[1]</sup> 

- Surface morphology depends on the composition of the etchant as well as other parameters like stirring & temperature 

- HF-H2O-Br2 solutions are isotropic etchants over a wide range 

   - of different concentrations of the reactants<sup>[2]</sup> 

- Anisotropic etching is achieved by addition of HBr to HF-H2OBr 2 mixtures, leading to random upright or inverted pyramids 

- During etching, formation of reactive Br-O-species is possible 

- • In this work, we focus on perbromic acid (HBrO4), its influence on silicon etch rates and generated surface morphologies
